Recollection: Inang

View with Adobe Aero

 I felt inspired to combine this photo of my grandma from 1961 with images that reminded me of the Philippines. While creating this piece I thought of the word my mom calls her, "Inang", which translates to "mother" in Tagalog, and I also wanted to represent the duality of mother nature. The landscape at the bottom and smoke at the top are images of the eruption of Mt. Pinatubo, a volcano which is located near my grandma's province, and the image on the right is a satellite view of a typhoon, both natural disasters my grandma has survived through. The assortment of native fruit includes coconuts, mangoes, calamansi, and an eggplant flower as symbols of resilience and abundance despite the challenges she faced. The seven flowers surrounding her head are the national flower of the Philippines and represent her four sons and three daughters.
